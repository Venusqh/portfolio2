Least Cost Pathway Analysis

This map presents a least-cost path analysis model used to identify optimal hiking trails and routes in Yellowstone National Park. The methodology involves a multi-step process that considers several key criteria, including land cover, slope, wolf density, distance to existing campsites and trails, and proximity to roads. The analysis was conducted using the 'Weighted Sum' tool to generate a surface cost raster, followed by the 'Cost Distance' and 'Cost Path' tools to determine and visualize the most efficient routes based on the cumulative travel cost.

Geocoding and Linear Referencing

Geocoding is the process of converting descriptive location information such as addresses, postal codes, or place names, into geographic coordinates. It enables the visualization of tabular data on a map, including plotting customer addresses, service locations, or incident reports.

Linear referencing is a spatial referencing method used to locate features along a linear path like roads, pipelines, or rivers, based on distance measurements from a known starting point. This technique is especially useful for infrastructure analysis, such as identifying maintenance zones or accident locations along highways.
